travelling

Travel is the movement of people between distant geographical locations. Travel can be done by foot, bicycle, automobile, train, boat, bus, airplane, ship or other means, with or without luggage, and can be one way or round trip.

Travelling in Bangladesh

Bangladesh offers a diverse range of travel experiences, from bustling city life in Dhaka to peaceful tea gardens in Sreemangal and the unique mangrove ecosystem of the Sundarbans. Popular attractions include Cox's Bazar beach, Saint Martin's Island, and historical sites like Ahsan Manzil and Lalbagh Fort. Visitors should be prepared for heat and humidity, and to respect local customs and traditions.

Cox's Bazar:

Known for its unbroken sandy beach stretching for over 120 kilometers, Cox's Bazar offers a classic beach experience with opportunities for water sports, seafood, and enjoying beautiful sunsets.


Dhaka:

Bangladesh's bustling capital city, Dhaka, offers a mix of historical sites like Lalbagh Fort and Ahsan Manzil, vibrant markets, and a rich cultural scene.

Sundarbans

This UNESCO World Heritage Site is the world's largest mangrove forest and a haven for wildlife, including the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger.

Rangamati:

Situated in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, Rangamati is known for its scenic beauty, including Kaptai Lake and the surrounding hills. It's also a place to experience the culture of indigenous communities.

Bandarban:

Another hill station in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, Bandarban boasts stunning mountain views, lush green landscapes, and opportunities for trekking and exploring waterfalls.

Saint Martin's Island:

The only coral island in Bangladesh, Saint Martin's offers a tranquil escape with opportunities for snorkeling, diving, and enjoying the laid-back island atmosphere.

Sreemangal:

This region is famous for its lush tea gardens and is a great place to experience the tea-making process and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Ratargul Swamp Forest:

A unique wetland ecosystem, Ratargul offers a chance to explore the forest by boat and experience its distinctive biodiversity.
